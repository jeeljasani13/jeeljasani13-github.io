The MHCI Capstone Project is an eight month long client facing project. Our client is a German Engineering Company - Bosch Ltd. We are currently investigating the security and privacy concerns around smart homes.

The capstone project entails in-depth user centered research followed by low, mid and high fidelity designs ending with working prototypes. We are currently in the research phase and will now be beginning with the generative design. Since this is an ongoing project, more information will be added over time.


A lot of the information here has been kept confidential due to a signed NDA.

The Problem

People everywhere now own smart devices. We're now even exposed to having multiple smart devices connecting with each other and making life easier in what we call smart homes. When we use all these devices, little do we know about what information of our personal lives we are actually giving out. Do we have control over this information? Do we want control over this information? What would an ideal smart home be like keeping in mind all the security and privacy concerns of the people?

Research

Our research was broken into three phases. We first started off with interviewing Experts. We then went on to interview users of the system and also sent out surveys. Our current and ongoing phase involves a research method called Cardigami.

Interviews with Experts

We interviewed subject matter experts to know about their knowledge of the field and get relevant insights from their research methods that could help us plan our process.

Interviews with Users

We then went on to do a round of interviews with our users to gauge their present mental model of how smart things work.

Cardigami

Cardigami was a method that I ideated as a mix of card sorting as well as business origami. Over time we worked on it as a team and came up with what was most useful for us. It was mainly intended to prime users in their comfort zone and them provoke them to help us generate more useful scenarios.
