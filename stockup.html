StockUp is an app designed for the Interaction Design Fundamentals class at Carnegie Mellon University. The project required us to conceive an original mobile service that provides tools and/or information to an ambulance driver/attendant accessing critical information while on the job.

We demonstrated our ability to sketch a better future based on the technology of today and the near future. A critical part of this assignment was cultivating understanding and empathy toward your users, and discovering a need or opportunity that doesn't exist.

The Problem

Time is crucial for EMTs.
A sudden cardiac arrest patient looses two million neurons for every minute their brain is without oxygen.
EMTs do not have a lot of time for secondary tasks like inventory management, but keeping track of what is and isn't in the ambulance is very important as otherwise they might not have a required medicine during an emergency.

Research

We started with idea generation. We created an affinity mapping of our ideas to find any overlap. From these ideas we picked our ten favorite ones and created a feasibility graph of those to see which idea would solve a genuine problem. We narrowed it down to four ideas which we got validated from an ambulance driver at Pittsburgh Paramedic. The driver gave us valuable insights on their day to day activities and problems they faced which helped us narrow down our scope.

The Selected Idea

StockUp is an inventory management assistant which keeps track of the ambulance inventory reducing the manual labor to be done by EMTs and saving them time to focus on more important tasks like saving lives. Here is a list of functionality it provides:

• Single sign-on functionality using fingerprint scanning.
• Ability to view the inventory status.
• Ability to view what items are expiring, expired, critically low or out of stock.
• Ability to view the different categories of items stored in an ambulance inventory.
• Ability to view detailed description of each inventory item eg: expiry date, quantity, photo etc.
• QR code scanner to allow users to add or remove items from the inventory.
• Feature to allow printing or emailing the order list from the app.
• Search option to find a particular item from the inventory.
• Sync up with the back-end inventory system.

Prototyping

After going through all the data gathered from our research, we decided on the main functions we wanted the app to include. We started designing the screens using paper prototypes. After getting substantial feedback from our peers we went on to create digital prototypes which were refined over time.
